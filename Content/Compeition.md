# Competition Safety

Competition is an extreemly fun expreience but also comes with many things to keep in mind. 

## Pits

The pits are where robots are when not on the field and where all the tools are that teams use at competition. It can be a very chatic place expessally when something when not planned. There are many rules in the pits (many of which are the same as machine shops). Some rules are constant for example no matter what is happening, safety glasses are required at **all times** no matter what is going on. The full set of rules can be found in section 7.3 of the offical FIRST Safety Manual

### Pit Admin

Pit Admin is the source of just about any bit of information at competition. If you have questions about anything, they will know the answer, or they will get you to the person that will know the right answer. There are some things that you are supposed to go to them for. For example if there is a battery spill you should go to Pit Admin so they can contact event managment incase the venue has something special that needs to be done.

## Breaks

Breaks are an extreemly importent thing at competition. They give you rest and can help prevent burnout. Competition is long and takes a lot of energy, that statment is even more true for Pit Crew and Drive team. Everyone should take breaks through out the day it could be going to the stand to watch some matches if you are in the pit all day, it could be going to the carrer fair if your competition has it. There are many options, but that is not the importent part, takes the breaks is.

Getting a break from what doing all day can also help prevent burn out. That can keep you more alert to what you are doing and can help prevent incidents.

## Safety Captain meetings

Safety Captain meetings are daily meetings (typicly in the mornings) which all the Safety Captains join together with the Safety Manager. Many things are discussed such as event specific situations for example if the layout of the event is not like others. It is importent to attend since you can learn many things event specific or not. You can also develop relationships with the Safety Manager and other Safety Captains
