# Personal Protective Equipment

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is equipment used to protect its users from physical harm. PPE is the last line of protection. PPE can include safety glasses/googles, gloves and steel toed boots but **NOT ALL** types of PPE can be used at the same time without risking harm.

## Examples of PPE

This list is not a complete list and there are much more PPE than listed here. These are just some of the more common kinds.

- Safety Goggles/Glasses | Protect eyes from flying debris  
  <img src="../Media/PPE/Safety Glasses.png" alt="Safety Glasses" />
- Gloves | Protects hands (and sometimes wrists depending on the gloves) from cuts and scrapes. **SHOULD NOT** be used with a rotary tool as you risk it getting caught in the tool bringing your hand with it.  
- Steel toed boots | Protects your toes from falling objects.  
- Ear plugs | Protects from hearing damage  
  <img src="../Media/PPE/Ear Plugs.png" alt="Ear plugs" />
- Resparaters | Protects from particles suspended in the air.

## When to use and not to use PPE

For the most part if you are not sure if you should use a piece of PPE it's better to use it than not. But it's always better to ask somebody that would know than take a guess. There are **EXCEPTIONS** to whether it is better to wear it or not though. Gloves should not be worn when using rotary tools such as drill, disk/belt sanders, bandsaws and more. These tools spin so if a glove touches the spinning part there is a chance it gets tangled bringing your hand along. Ear plugs should only be worn when the surrounding area is loud. If the area is quiet you should not be wearing ear plugs so you can hear what is going on around you.

On the other hand, some other PPE is always better to wear than not. For example it's always better to wear safety glasses/goggles than not.
