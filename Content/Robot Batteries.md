# Robot Batteries

A robot weights about 13 pounds (~5.8kg) and it is filled with acid. Not to say that you should be scared of them, they are "non spillable" meaning that the battery is sealed and the acid wont just come out the case needs to fail.

## Composition
The content of the batteries are composed of Lead, Lead Dioxide and Tin, (for specifics check the SDS). That need to clean it has you don’t want lead sitting on the floor.

## Handling

The handling of batteries is not some complex procedure. Its at least one had that has a secure grip on the case of the battery, that is it! As long as you don’t hold it from the wires you will be good.

## Spills

Even though the batteries are "non spillable" does not mean circumstances can arise leading to the lead acid spilling. When a battery spills the first step is to remain calm. After that get all others away from the spill. Once the area is get a battery spill kit and put on the gloves. Then open the baking soda and put it on any thing the acid touched. Then take the battery and put in the plastic bin or plastic bag to contain any more acid that spills before disposal.
